Frequently Asked Legal Questions

Answers to questions commonly searched by individuals, businesses, and NRIs seeking legal help in Kerala - covering High Court procedure, property, family law, criminal law, consumer matters, and more.

Commonly Asked Legal Questions

These questions are commonly searched on Google by people seeking legal help in Kerala. If your question is not answered here, contact us directly to speak with an advocate.

High Court Procedure

Cases before the High Court of Kerala are filed through petitions such as writ petitions, appeals, or original petitions depending on the nature of the dispute.

Constitutional matters, civil appeals, criminal appeals, service matters, and challenges to government actions.

Yes, in appropriate circumstances under inherent jurisdiction of the High Court.

A writ petition can challenge unlawful government actions or protect fundamental rights under the Constitution of India.

Family Law

Divorce timelines vary depending on whether the case is contested or mutual and the complexity of issues such as custody or property disputes.

Protection orders, residence orders, and maintenance can be sought under the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005.

NRI Representation

Yes. Non-Resident Indians can initiate or defend cases through authorized legal representation without being physically present.

Through succession proceedings and property partition cases.

Property Law

Title deeds, encumbrance certificates, identification documents, tax receipts, and related registration forms.

Through proper title verification, document scrutiny, and encumbrance certificate analysis under the Transfer of Property Act, 1882 and Registration Act, 1908.

Legal remedies include injunction suits, declaration suits, or mediation depending on the dispute.

Criminal Law

Legal action may be initiated under the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881.

Bail is a legal release from custody pending trial in criminal cases under the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023.

Consumer Law

A complaint can be filed before the Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019.

Alternative Dispute Resolution

Arbitration is a private dispute resolution mechanism under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.

Mediation is a voluntary dispute resolution method that allows parties to settle disputes without lengthy litigation.

Property and Real Estate

The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 protects homebuyers and regulates real estate projects.

Corporate and Financial Law

Banks may initiate proceedings before the Debt Recovery Tribunal under the Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002.

Yes, through arbitration or mediation under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.

Choosing a Lawyer

Choose a lawyer with experience in litigation, strong legal research skills, and a transparent approach to client representation.
