Courts and Tribunals We Practise Before

From the High Court of Kerala to specialised tribunals and arbitral forums - Intra Legem Lawyers represents clients across the full hierarchy of India's judicial and quasi-judicial institutions.

Our practice extends well beyond a single court. Whether your matter sits before a constitutional bench, a district civil court, a consumer commission, or an arbitral tribunal, we bring the same depth of preparation and strategic advocacy to every forum. Understanding which institution has jurisdiction over your dispute is the first step toward resolving it - and it is a step we take with you.

Our Jurisdictional Reach

Thousands of cases handled across 10+ courts and tribunals throughout Kerala and India.

The firm practises regularly before the following courts, tribunals, commissions, and alternative dispute resolution forums. Each institution operates under a distinct statutory framework, and our attorneys are experienced in the procedural rules, filing requirements, and advocacy standards of every forum listed below.

Constitutional and High Courts

The apex court within the state judicial hierarchy for constitutional, civil, criminal, and writ matters.

  • High Court of Kerala

Civil Courts

First-instance and appellate civil courts handling property disputes, injunctions, recovery suits, and all civil matters across the state.

  • District and Subordinate Courts across Kerala

Family Courts

Statutory courts established under the Family Courts Act 1984, handling divorce, maintenance, custody, guardianship, and matrimonial property matters.

  • Family Courts

Consumer Forums

Quasi-judicial commissions under the Consumer Protection Act 2019 adjudicating disputes between consumers and service providers or manufacturers.

  • Consumer Disputes Redressal Commissions

Specialised Tribunals

Statutory tribunals with subject-matter jurisdiction over corporate insolvency, debt recovery, real estate regulation, and central service matters.

  • National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT)
  • Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT)
  • Real Estate Regulatory Authority (RERA)
  • Central Administrative Tribunal (CAT)
  • Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal (MACT)

Alternative Dispute Resolution

Private and institutional arbitration proceedings and mediation forums providing binding and non-binding dispute resolution outside the court system.

  • Arbitral Tribunals and mediation forums

If your matter falls before a forum not listed here, contact us - we will assess jurisdiction and advise on the appropriate forum and course of action.

Not Sure Which Court Has Jurisdiction?

Identifying the right forum is often the most consequential early decision in any dispute. Our attorneys will assess your matter, explain which court or tribunal has jurisdiction, and outline your options - clearly and without unnecessary complexity.
