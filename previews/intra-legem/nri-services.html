Distance should not compromise your legal rights in India. We provide dedicated legal services to Non-Resident Indians - handling your court proceedings, property documentation, family matters, and succession disputes through a structured remote consultation model, so you stay informed and in control without the disruption of repeated travel.

Matrimonial Disputes and Divorce Proceedings

Divorce petitions, maintenance applications, child custody and guardianship proceedings, matrimonial property disputes, and domestic violence matters - filed and argued before Family Courts and the High Court of Kerala on your behalf.

  • Hindu Marriage Act 1955

  • Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act 2005

Property Disputes and Land Documentation

Title verification, injunction suits, land boundary and encroachment disputes, recovery of possession, property documentation review, sale deed and settlement deed drafting, and registration matters - handled end-to-end without requiring your physical presence at every stage.

  • Transfer of Property Act 1882
  • Code of Civil Procedure 1908
  • Registration Act 1908
  • Kerala land laws and property registration statutes

Succession and Inheritance Issues

Succession certificates, probate proceedings, partition suits, will disputes, intestate inheritance claims, and legal heir documentation - ensuring your share of property and assets is protected and legally secured.

  • Indian Succession Act 1925
  • Hindu Succession Act 1956
  • Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Court Representation Without Your Travel to India

We appear before the High Court of Kerala, District Courts, Family Courts, Consumer Commissions, and Tribunals as your authorised legal representatives. Using a Power of Attorney or appropriate procedural authorisation, we file applications, attend hearings, and keep you informed - so proceedings move forward without your presence in India.

How It Works

A Clear Process - From Your First Call to Final Resolution

We have designed our NRI consultation model to be straightforward, transparent, and time-zone considerate. Here is how we engage with overseas clients from the first contact to ongoing representation.

  1. 01

    Initial Phone or Video Consultation

    We begin with a call - phone or video - at a time that works across your time zone. We'll listen to your situation in full, identify the legal issues at stake, and give you clear initial direction on the viable options and the correct forum in India.

  2. 02

    We Review Your Documents Remotely

    Once you share the relevant documents - property papers, court notices, family certificates, agreements, or any prior legal correspondence - we'll review them thoroughly and advise you on the legal position, risks, and recommended course of action.

  3. 03

    We Draft the Authorisation and Engagement

    We'll walk you through everything you need to sign - Power of Attorney, vakalatnama, or any other authorisation document - so we are formally empowered to file and appear on your behalf. We prepare the paperwork; you execute it from wherever you are.

  4. 04

    We File and Appear on Your Behalf

    With authorisation in place, we handle all filings, court appearances, and procedural steps. You receive regular updates on hearing dates, orders passed, and next steps - without needing to be present in India at any stage unless the court specifically requires it.

  5. 05

    We Keep You Informed Throughout

    Legal proceedings in India can take time. We keep the communication clear and consistent - updating you after each hearing, explaining developments in plain language, and advising on any decisions you need to make along the way.
