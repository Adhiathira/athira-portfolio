2. Scope and Purpose of This Policy

This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect when you use this website, why we collect it, how we use and protect it, and what rights you have under applicable Indian law, including the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ('DPDPA 2023'). This policy applies to all visitors, prospective clients, and individuals who submit inquiries or contact us through this website. It does not govern personal data exchanged as part of a formal attorney-client retainer, which is subject to separate engagement terms and professional confidentiality obligations.

3. Personal Data We Collect

We collect only the personal data you voluntarily provide to us. This may include:

(a) Contact inquiries: Your name, email address, phone number, and the content of your message when you submit our contact form or send us an email.

(b) Consultation requests: Details of your legal matter that you choose to share when requesting an initial consultation by phone, video call, or in person.

(c) Website usage data: Basic technical data such as your IP address, browser type, device type, pages visited, and approximate geographic region, collected automatically through our web hosting infrastructure. We do not use Google Analytics or any third-party analytics platform at present. If analytics tools are added in future, this policy will be updated before deployment.

We do not collect special category personal data (such as religious beliefs, political opinions, or health data) through this website. If you voluntarily share such information in a message, we will treat it with the same protections as other personal data.

4. How We Use Your Personal Data

We use the personal data you provide for the following purposes:

(a) To respond to your inquiry or consultation request;

(b) To assess whether we are in a position to advise or represent you and to communicate initial legal direction;

(c) To schedule and conduct consultations by phone, video call, or in person;

(d) To comply with our legal and professional obligations as advocates enrolled with the Bar Council of Kerala;

(e) To maintain records of communications for the purposes of professional accountability.

We do not use your personal data for automated decision-making, profiling, or any commercial purpose unrelated to your legal inquiry.

5. Legal Basis for Processing

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, we process your personal data on the following bases:

(a) Consent: When you submit a contact form or voluntarily send us a message, you consent to us using your contact information to respond to you. You may withdraw this consent at any time by writing to us at intralegemlawyers@gmail.com; withdrawal of consent will not affect any processing already carried out.

(b) Legitimate use: To the extent permitted under the DPDPA 2023, we may process certain data to fulfil functions reasonably expected in the course of operating a professional legal services practice, including maintaining records of inquiries received.

6. How We Store and Protect Your Data

This website is hosted on Vercel's infrastructure, which stores data on servers that may be located outside India. Vercel operates under industry-standard security certifications and implements appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data. By submitting your information through this website, you acknowledge that your data may be processed on Vercel's infrastructure.

Contact form submissions and inquiries sent to our email address are stored within our email system. We implement access controls and maintain communications only for as long as necessary for the purpose for which they were collected or as required by applicable law.

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with any third party for marketing or commercial purposes.

7. Disclosure of Your Personal Data

We do not disclose your personal data to third parties except in the following circumstances:

(a) Where disclosure is required by a court order, statute, or other legal obligation;

(b) To our professional indemnity insurer or legal counsel, to the extent necessary for the conduct of any proceeding;

(c) To service providers who assist us in operating this website (such as our hosting provider, Vercel), who are contractually bound to process personal data only on our instructions and in accordance with applicable law;

(d) With your explicit consent.

We do not transfer your personal data to any overseas entity for the purpose of marketing, analytics, or data processing, except as inherent in the use of our hosting infrastructure described in Section 6 above.

8. Your Rights Under the DPDPA 2023

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data processed by us:

(a) Right to information: You have the right to obtain confirmation of whether we process your personal data and a summary of the personal data held.

(b) Right to correction and erasure: You have the right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data, and erasure of personal data that is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, subject to any retention obligations we may have under applicable law or professional rules.

(c) Right to grievance redressal: You have the right to have your grievances addressed by us in a timely manner.

(d) Right to nominate: You have the right to nominate another individual to exercise your rights under the DPDPA 2023 in the event of your death or incapacity.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us in writing at intralegemlawyers@gmail.com. We will respond within a reasonable time and in accordance with the timelines prescribed under the DPDPA 2023 and applicable rules notified thereunder. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may file a complaint with the Data Protection Board of India once it is constituted and operational under the DPDPA 2023.

9. Cookies

This website does not currently use cookies for tracking, analytics, or advertising purposes. Essential session-level cookies that may be set by the web hosting infrastructure for basic functionality do not collect personal data and are not used for profiling. If cookies are introduced in a future version of this website, we will update this policy and, where required, obtain your consent before setting non-essential cookies.

10. Links to Third-Party Websites

This website may contain links to external websites, including court portals, statutory authority websites, or publicly available legal resources. This Privacy Policy applies only to this website. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of any external website, and we encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party site you visit.

11. Children's Privacy

This website is not directed at children under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. If you believe that a minor has submitted personal data to us through this website, please contact us at intralegemlawyers@gmail.com and we will take steps to delete it promptly.

We retain personal data submitted through contact forms and inquiry communications for as long as reasonably necessary to address your inquiry and for a reasonable period thereafter to maintain professional records, unless a longer period is required by law. Hosting and server logs are retained in accordance with Vercel's data retention policies. Where you request erasure of your personal data, we will comply to the extent that retention is not required by applicable statute, court order, or Bar Council rules.
